Foreign Studies  
 
 Haloul  et al. (2024) conducted a comprehensive review of Project Management 
Information Systems (PMIS) across industries such as construction, IT, finance, and 
manufacturing. Their study emphasized that PMIS allows managers to plan, monitor, and 
evaluate project s more efficiently. The findings suggest that web -based PMIS enhances 
organizational performance by centralizing project data and promoting professional project 
delivery.  
 
 In a separate work, Mortadza and Rizal (2024) examined the role of digital Project 
Management Systems (PMS) in improving project reporting. They argued that traditional 
reporting methods often lead to delays and miscommunication, while PMS solutions enable  
real-time visibility and automation that strengthen decision -making and collaboration. Despite 
these benefits, they also identified barriers to adoption, such as integration difficulties, 
cybersecurity concerns, and resistance to organizational change.  
 
 Similarly, Singh and Jankovitz (2018) explored the importance of project management 
skills in preparing professionals for modern workplaces. They concluded that project 
management tools not only support accountability and teamwork but also improve 
communic ation within collaborative projects. Their study recommended strategies such as 
structured scheduling, use of communication frameworks, and accountability mechanisms to 
ensure project success.

 2.4 Definition of Terms  
 
To provide clarity and consistency, the following key terms are defined as used in this study:  
 
Project Management System  (PMS) – A software tool that assists in planning, assigning, 
monitoring, and evaluating projects and tasks within an organization.  
 
Task Management  – The process of creating, assigning, tracking, and completing tasks within 
a project to ensure timely execution and accountability.  
 
Web -Based System  – A software application that runs on a web server and can be accessed 
through a browser using the internet, providing flexibility and accessibility across devices.  
 
Dashboard  – A visual interface in the system that displays real -time project information such 
as task progress, deadlines, and assigned responsibilities.  
 
Collaboration Tools  – Features in the system that allow team members to communicate, share 
files, and work together efficiently on assigned tasks.  
 
Centralized Database  – A structured digital storage where all project data, including tasks, 
updates, and reports, are securely stored and accessed by authorized users.  
 
Role -Based Access Control  (RBAC) – A security method where system access and 
permissions are based on user roles, such as manager or team member.  
 
Notifications/Alerts  – Automated reminders generated by the system to inform users of 
upcoming deadlines, overdue tasks, or updates in project status.  
 
Progress Monitoring  – The continuous tracking and assessment of project activities and task 
completion to ensure alignment with goals and deadlines.  
 
Accountability  – The responsibility of each user to complete assigned tasks on time, made 
visible through system tracking and reporting features.

3.1 Research Design  
 This study employed a Quantitative –Developmental Research Design to guide the 
creation, implementation, and evaluation of the Project Management System for Rokshu 
Corporation with Data Analytics. The quantitative component focused on collecting 
measurable data from respondents to assess the effectiveness, usability, and reliability of the 
developed system, while the developmental aspect concentrated on the actual design and system 
construction.  
 
 The developmental phase followed the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), 
specifically the Waterfall Model, which includes stages such as requirements analysis, system 
design, coding, testing, and implementation. This ensured that each stage of system 
development was systematically and sequentially executed to meet the company’s operational 
needs.  
 
 The quantitative phase involved the distribution of survey questionnaires to selected 
employees and managers of Rokshu Corporation. Their responses were statistically analyzed 
using tools such as Weighted Mean and Standard Deviation to determine the level of user 
satisfaction and consistency of feedback. These numerical results provided a factual basis for 
evaluating the system’s overall performance. In this study, the researchers implemented several 
strategies to further expand the data and analyze it pro perly for an accurate and relevant project 
management system for Rokshu Corporation. Combining the methods was necessary to ensure

 By combining quantitative evaluation with the developmental process, the study

approach ensured that the Project Management System not only met technical requi rements 
but also produced measurable improvements in project coordination, task management, and 
data -driven decision -making within Rokshu Corporatio n.

3.2.1 Data Gathering Technique  
 The researchers utilized a survey questionnaire as the main data -gathering 
instrument to collect quantitative data regarding the effectiveness and performance of 
the developed Project Management System for Rokshu Corporation with Data 
Analytics. The questi onnaire was created and distributed using Google Forms to ensure 
accessibility, accuracy, and convenience for respondents.  
 
 The instrument was designed based on selected ISO/IEC 25010 software 
quality characteristics, which include functionality, usability, efficiency, reliability, 
maintainability, and overall satisfaction. Each category contained specific statements 
that allow ed respondents to rate their level of agreement and satisfaction with the 
developed system.  

42 
  
 To ensure systematic data collection, the researchers followed a structured 
procedure. The survey link was shared with the employees and managers of Rokshu 
Corporation who directly participated in the system testing and evaluation. These 
respondents were c hosen because of their familiarity with the company’s project 
management process and their involvement in daily operational activities.  
 
A five -point Likert scale was used to measure the respondents’ level of agreement for 
each statement:  
5 – Strongly Agree  4 – Agree  3 – Neutral  2 – Disagree  1 – Strongly Disagree.  
 
 The responses were automatically recorded and tabulated using Google Sheets, 
providing a reliable dataset for analysis. The researchers then applied Weighted Mean 
and Standard Deviation to evaluate the respondents’ feedback and determine the 
system’s level  of user satisfaction, consistency, and effectiveness.  
 
 The questionnaire also included an open -ended section where participants 
could provide comments and suggestions for possible improvements to the system. This 
combination of quantitative data and qualitative insights helped the researchers gain a 
deeper und erstanding of the system’s strengths and areas for enhancement.  
 
 Overall, this data -gathering process ensured that the evaluation of the Project 
Management System was objective, systematic, and data -driven, reflecting the actual 
experiences and perspectives of its intended users at Rokshu Corporation.
